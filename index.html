Accuracy without interpretability is incomplete science — I build models that explain themselves.

— Research philosophy, Md. Faysal Hossan

I am Md. Faysal Hossan, a final-year undergraduate researcher in Computer Science & Engineering at Daffodil International University, Dhaka, Bangladesh. My work sits at the intersection of deep learning, computer vision, and Explainable AI — with a focus on building transparent, deployment-ready models for agricultural and medical image analysis.

My undergraduate thesis, LXViT, proposes a hybrid hierarchical architecture fusing Xception's deep convolutional feature extraction with Vision Transformer attention mechanisms for multi-class lemon leaf disease classification. The framework incorporates both LIME and SHAP post-hoc explainability methods to ensure clinical and agronomic interpretability — addressing one of the core limitations of black-box deep learning in high-stakes domains.

As a Research Assistant under Asst. Prof. MD Masum Billah (DIU), I have contributed to 2 Q1/Scopus-indexed Elsevier journal papers and 12 peer-reviewed conference papers spanning IEEE ICCIT 2025, IEEE QPAIN 2026, Springer ANTIC 2025, and BIM 2025. My research spans plant disease detection (lemon, pomelo, jackfruit, guava, papaya, lychee, medicinal leaves), NLP-based mental health detection, medical image analysis (cervical cancer, sperm morphology), and social behaviour prediction — reflecting both domain breadth and methodological depth.

I have curated and publicly released 14 open-access datasets on Mendeley Data — covering multi-crop leaf diseases, medicinal plants, clinical sperm analysis, and social surveys — contributing directly to the reproducibility and advancement of agricultural AI research globally.

Beyond research, I serve as Joint Lead Executive of the DIU Cyber Security Club (2025–2026) and as an Executive Member of the 4IR Research Cell, where I mentor junior researchers in academic writing, dataset curation, and paper submission workflows.

Research

Research Interests

🌿 Agricultural AI & Plant Disease Detection

Deep learning models for automatic detection and classification of plant diseases from leaf images, targeting real-world deployment in South Asian agriculture.

👁 Computer Vision & Hybrid Architectures

Hybrid CNN-ViT frameworks, transfer learning, image classification, and CycleGAN-based data augmentation for imbalanced agricultural datasets.

🧠 Explainable AI (XAI)

LIME and SHAP-based model interpretability techniques to make deep learning decisions transparent and trustworthy for clinicians and policymakers.

🔬 Medical Image Analysis

Deep learning-based analysis of clinical medical images including sperm morphology (CSAD dataset), cell classification, and disease biomarker detection — bridging AI research with healthcare applications.

💬 NLP & Mental Health AI

Transformer models, LoRA fine-tuning, and low-resource NLP for mental health detection from Bangla and English social media text.

Thesis

LXViT: A Hybrid Hierarchical Xception-ViT Framework for Lemon Leaf Disease Classification with Multi-Method Explainable AI Integration

Undergraduate thesis combining Xception (deep CNN) feature extraction with Vision Transformer (ViT) hierarchical attention mechanisms for lemon leaf disease classification. Validated using both LIME and SHAP post-hoc explainability frameworks for real-world trust and transparency. Supervised by MD Masum Billah (Asst. Prof., DIU).
